About Otter Creek

Otter creek is one of the larger rivers in the state of Vermont, roughly measuring around 112 miles long. Starting at fort Cassin point on Lake Champlain, it runs through both Rutland and Addison county, and through some of Vermont's major towns/cities, including Wallingford, Rutland, Brandon, Middlebury and Vergennes. The creek is also home to many types of fish species such as brook trout, northern pike, smallmouth bass and fallfish.

Leave No Trace!

While we encourage you to enjoy partaking in limitless recreational activities along Otter Creek, please remember that this is a unique ecosystem that is home to a diverse range of animals. If you visit this area, please do your part by disposing of waste properly and picking up litter whenever possible. Leave no trace!
