This project was created by Max Solter, Marissa Flynn and Eleanor Downie with the purpose to provide easily accessible information about points of access, obstacles, as well as manmade and historic features along the creek, in order to remedy this, we have created this web map and accompanying static maps to show otter creek’s hidden and underappreciated features. We hope this map will encourage recreationists to enjoy the area!

Environmental Notice

Please remember that the environment is fragile, follow the leave no trace rule. Remember the dams located along this are a central part of Otter Creek’s ecology, if you are on the river do not cut through them!

About Otter Creek

Otter creek is one of the larger rivers in the state of Vermont, roughly measuring around 112 miles. It runs through both Rutland and Addison county starting at fort Cassin point, Lake Champlain, and through some of Vermont's major towns/cities, including Wallingford, Rutland, Brandon, Middlebury and Vergennes. The creek is also home to many types of fish species such as brook trout, northern pike, smallmouth bass and fallfish.
